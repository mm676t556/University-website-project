Faculty of Mass Communication

Prof. Naeala Emaraa

I am proud to join the family of Pharos University in Alexandria, an ancient university that is truly one of the important pillars of education in the Arab Republic of Egypt, and I was happy to be chosen as Dean of the Faculty of Mass Communication, to have the honor of working among an elite group of academics specialized in the various fields of media, which truly reflect and keep pace with developments. Accelerating technology in the field of media work, to graduate students capable of serious and distinguished competition in the labor market, calling on God Almighty to grant us all success in shouldering this responsibility in order to achieve the university’s goals of excellence and commitment to media education standards locally and internationally.

Study Programs
The Faculty of Mass Communication – Pharos University awards a bachelor’s degree in mass communication, accredited and equivalent by the Supreme Council of Egyptian Universities and the Ministry of Higher Education.
    First: Bylaw of the faculty of Mass Communication for the academic year 2023 Specialization begins in the second year in the following academic programs:
  • Digital Media Platforms Program
  • Public Relations, Advertising and Marketing communications Program
  • Digital Broadcasting & Television production program
    Second: Bylaw of the faculty of Mass Communication for the academic year 2018 Specialization begins in the second year in the following academic programs:
  • Digital Journalism and Publishing Program
  • Public Relations and Marketing Communications Program
  • Radio and TV program
Why Study mass communication at PUA?

The Faculty of Mass Communication is considered one of the leading colleges in media education. It includes a distinguished elite of faculty members. It considers quality standards in the educational process and is interested in media training in various media specializations, keeping pace with modern technological developments in the field of media, and uses the latest learning strategies such as: active learning, interactive learning, e-learning, cooperative learning, and practical training. The existence of cooperation protocols with the largest media institutions. Hosting a group of senior media professionals in media institutions to train students and link the theoretical aspect with the practical aspect. The faculty includes several radio and television studios, which were designed in a scientific manner that suits the technical needs of radio and television production, a press laboratory, a news editing room, and a laboratory to produce publications and public relations leaflets.
